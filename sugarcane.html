Sugarcane Farming in KwaZulu-Natal

South Africa is one of the world's leading sugarcane producers, and KwaZulu-Natal's subtropical climate provides ideal conditions for high-yield cultivation. ShellNest Sugarcane Farming leverages this natural advantage with modern farming practices and deep respect for the land.

Our operation focuses on sustainable sugarcane cultivation, supplying raw cane to South Africa's sugar mills and contributing to the nation's sugar, ethanol, and bio-energy value chains.

We align with the South African Sugarcane Research Institute (SASRI) guidelines, ensuring optimal yields while preserving soil health for future generations.

Farming Calendar

From Planting to Harvest

Sugarcane is a perennial crop with a 12-18 month growth cycle. Our farming calendar ensures optimal yields through strategic timing.

Feb-Mar

Land Preparation

Soil testing, pH adjustment, deep ploughing, and ridging. Irrigation infrastructure checks and repair ahead of the growing season.

Mar-May

Planting Season

Seedcane selection from SASRI-approved varieties. Mechanical or manual planting in prepared rows with optimal spacing for the region.

May-Oct

Growth & Maintenance

Fertiliser application, weed management, pest monitoring (particularly Eldana borer), and irrigation scheduling during the dry months.

Oct-Dec

Ripening & Testing

Sucrose content monitoring begins. Chemical ripeners may be applied to maximise sugar content before harvest.

Apr-Dec

Harvest Season

Mechanical and manual harvesting. Cane delivered to contracted mill within 48 hours of cutting for optimal sucrose extraction.

Post

Ratoon Management

Trash management after harvest. Ratoon crops produce 5-8 subsequent harvests before replanting is required.

Value Chain

Beyond Raw Sugar

Sugarcane is one of the most versatile crops, producing multiple value streams from a single harvest.

Raw Sugar

Primary product delivered to regional mills for processing into white and brown sugar for domestic and export markets.

Bio-Ethanol

Molasses by-product processed into bio-ethanol for blending with petroleum fuels and industrial alcohol production.

Bio-Energy

Bagasse fibre burned for co-generation of electricity at sugar mills, contributing to South Africa's renewable energy mix.

Our Approach

Sustainable Farming Practices

Water Conservation

Drip and micro-sprinkler irrigation systems minimise water usage. Mulching with harvested trash retains soil moisture and reduces runoff.

Integrated Pest Management

Biological control of Eldana borer through habitat management and natural predators. Minimal chemical intervention philosophy.

Community Employment

Seasonal and permanent employment for local communities. Skills development and training programmes for farm workers.

Soil Health

Green manure cover crops, controlled traffic farming, and regular soil analysis ensure long-term fertility and prevent degradation.
